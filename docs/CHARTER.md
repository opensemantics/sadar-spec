This Charter is a companion document to the SADAR specification. It

## 1. Purpose

This Charter describes how the OpenSemantics.org program is organized,
how the SADAR specification is stewarded and developed, how
contributions are accepted, and how the SADAR Certification Program
operates. Where this Charter addresses governance process, it does so
as a supplement to the Community Specification Governance Policy 1.0.

---

## 2. The OpenSemantics.org Program

**2.1. Nature.** OpenSemantics.org is a publication and stewardship
program operated by Cognita AI Inc. It is a brand and program, not a
separate legal entity, foundation, or membership corporation. References
to "OpenSemantics.org" in the SADAR specification and its companion
documents mean this program as operated by Cognita AI Inc.

**2.2. Scope of the brand.** OpenSemantics.org name is associated with a collection of publications, best practices, standards, etc. under the broad theme of Trustworthy AI.  It is anticipated that, within OpenSemantics.org, various workgroups and projects may emerge.  The first such project is the SADAR Specification.

Therefore, OpenSemantics.org is the overarching brand under which these activities occur.

The OpenSemantics.org name is used solely
for the SADAR specification and its companion documents. It does not
denote an independent standards-development organization, and no
representation to the contrary is authorized.

## 3. Stewardship

**3.1. Steward.** Cognita AI Inc. ("Cognita") is the founder, funder,
and current Steward of the SADAR specification as a project beneath OpenSemantics.org.

The goal of OpenSemantics.org is the adoption of certain projects into formalized standards.  Should that occur, stewardship may transfer to a generally accepted standards body.

**3.2. Permanence.** Stewardship of SADAR is not transferable through the ordinary governance process, is not subject to transfer or assignment by any Participant, Maintainer, Editor, or member, and is not altered by the introduction of any membership or participation model under Section 8. The sole means by which stewardship may transfer is a voluntary act of the Steward, in its discretion, to a generally accepted standards body in connection with formal standardization, as contemplated in Section 3.1.

**3.3. Steward responsibilities.** The Steward maintains the
specification repository, funds its operation, designates the
Maintainer(s) and Editor(s), operates the SADAR Certification Program,
and owns and administers the SADAR&trade; mark as set forth in the
OpenSemantics.org Trademark Policy.

Any, and all, continuance of funding, support, or other activities by Cognita AI Inc are solely at its discretion and may be terminated without notice for any reason.

## 5. Working Group Stage

**5.1. Foundational stage.** SADAR is currently developed at a
foundational, single-steward stage. The Steward, acting as Maintainer
and Editor, originates and curates the specification.

**5.2. Consensus at this stage.** Under Governance Policy Section 2.1,
the Maintainer determines consensus. While the Working Group remains at
the foundational stage, consensus determinations and editorial decisions
are made by the Steward in its capacity as Maintainer and Editor.

**5.3. Maturation.** As participation grows, the Steward intends to
broaden active participation in decision-making consistent with the
openness, balance, lack-of-dominance, and consideration-of-objections
principles of the Governance Policy. Broadening participation does not
transfer stewardship (Section 3.2).

**5.4. Appeals.** Appeals follow Governance Policy Section 2.2. At the
foundational stage, appeals are considered by the Steward as Maintainer,
in good faith, with a written response within a reasonable time.

## 7. The SADAR Certification Program

**7.1. Purpose.** The Certification Program provides a consistent basis
for evaluating and recognizing conformant SADAR implementations.

**NOTE** The certification process specifics will be defined at a later date.  The information provided in this section serves to frame the concept of Certification.

**7.2. Conformance tiers.** Consistent with the SADAR Specification
Scope, the program recognizes three tiers:

| Tier | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Self-Declared Conformance | The implementer asserts that its implementation satisfies all normative SADAR requirements. Self-declaration implies no review or attestation by OpenSemantics.org and confers no authorization to use the SADAR certification mark. |
| Certification | Conformance has been evaluated against the SADAR conformance test suite by OpenSemantics.org or a delegated conformance body. Certification is required for certification-mark authorization. |
| Authorized | Authorization represents conformance to certain operational and trust aspects related to Registry Federation.  To make registries available for federation, the Home, or originating, registry must be Authorized via the approved authorization program.  Certification is a prerequisite for Authorization.  An Authorized Registry may use the Authorization mark. |

**7.3. Certification and mark authorization.** Certification of conformant implementation, and authorization to use the SADAR
certification mark, are granted in writing and governed by the
OpenSemantics.org Trademark Policy.

**7.4. Certification is not a prerequisite to implement.** Certification and mark authorization govern use of the SADAR marks only. The right to implement the SADAR specification arises from the Community Specification License 1.0 on its own terms and does not depend on certification, mark authorization, or any commercial relationship with the Steward.

**7.5. Neutrality.** The Certification Program is open to all
implementers on equal terms, without preference as to cloud service
provider, model provider, hosting model, deployment topology, or
commercial relationship with the Steward.

The steward, or its agents, reserve the right to charge reasonable fees, at their sole discretion, to perform and maintain certification and authorization programs and for performing the assessments.

## 8. Participation and Funding

**8.1. Funding.** Cognita funds the operation of OpenSemantics.org and
the development of the SADAR specification.  Continuance of funding and funding amount is at the sole discretion of Cognita AI Inc.


**8.2. Membership (future).** The Steward may introduce a membership or
sponsorship model to support participation and funding. Any such model
will be documented in this Charter. Financial contribution does not
confer control over the specification, stewardship, or certification
outcomes, and does not alter the permanence of stewardship under
Section 3.2.

**8.3. Commercial implementations.** The Steward and third parties may
develop commercial implementations of SADAR. Commercial implementations
receive no preferential treatment in the specification, in governance,
or in the certification process.
