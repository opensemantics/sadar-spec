# OpenSemantics.org Trademark Policy

**Document:** OpenSemantics.org Trademark Policy
**Version:** 1.0
**Steward:** Cognita AI Inc.
**License:** Community Specification License 1.0

---

This Policy is a companion document to the SADAR specification. It
governs use of the SADAR™ name, the SADAR composite marks, and the SADAR
logos. It is distinct from, and operates alongside, the
intellectual-property license granted by the Community Specification
License 1.0.

---

## 1. Purpose

This Policy sets out how the SADAR marks may and may not be used, and how
authorization to use the SADAR logos is obtained.

---

## 2. Ownership and Status

**2.1. Owner.** SADAR™, OpenSemantics SADAR™, and SADAR Project™ are
trademarks of Cognita AI Inc. ("Cognita"), stewarded through the
OpenSemantics.org program.

**2.2. Common-law marks.** SADAR™, OpenSemantics SADAR™, and SADAR
Project™ are asserted and used as common-law trademarks. Cognita does
not, at this time, assert federal or other registered-mark status and
uses the ™ designation accordingly. Cognita reserves all rights in the
marks under applicable law, including the right to seek registration in
the future.

**2.3. Mark posture.** The composite marks (OpenSemantics SADAR™, SADAR
Project™) and the SADAR logos are the principal protectable marks. The
term "SADAR" alone is used as shorthand and referentially to identify the
specification; this Policy does not assert exclusive rights in the bare
term beyond what applicable law provides.

**2.4. Administration.** The marks are administered through the
OpenSemantics.org program (see Charter, Section 2). OpenSemantics.org is
not a separate legal entity; the owner of the marks is Cognita AI Inc.

---

## 3. Marks Covered

This Policy applies to (collectively, the "Marks"):

- the word mark **SADAR** and the composite marks **OpenSemantics SADAR**
  and **SADAR Project**;
- the **SADAR Certification logo** and the **SADAR Authorization logo**,
  when and as issued; and
- the **OpenSemantics.org** name.

---

## 4. The IP License Does Not Grant Trademark Rights

**4.1.** The Community Specification License 1.0 grants copyright and
patent rights to implement the SADAR specification. It does not grant any
right to use the Marks; Section 5 of that license expressly grants no
trademark rights.

**4.2.** The attribution that the Community Specification License 1.0
requires for derivative works of the specification (its name, version,
and source) is distinct from, and does not by itself authorize,
trademark use under this Policy.

---

## 5. Permitted Referential (Nominative) Use

You may use the word "SADAR" without prior permission to refer truthfully
to the specification, provided the use:

- is accurate and not misleading;
- does not imply endorsement, sponsorship, certification, authorization,
  or affiliation that does not exist;
- does not present the Marks more prominently than your own name or
  marks;
- does not alter, abbreviate, or restyle the Marks; and
- includes appropriate attribution to the SADAR specification where
  practical.

Examples of permitted referential use include: "implements the SADAR
specification," "compatible with SADAR," "built on SADAR," and "supports
SADAR."

---

## 6. Use of the Marks

The SADAR Certification and SADAR Authorization tiers, and the process by
which they are granted, are defined in the Charter (Section 7). This
section governs only how the corresponding Marks may be used.

**6.1. Logos are grant-only.** The SADAR Certification logo and the SADAR
Authorization logo may be used solely by parties to whom they have been
granted under the SADAR Certification Program and the Registry
Authorization Program (see Charter, Section 7), and only in accordance
with the terms of that grant. No other party may use either logo.

**6.2. Self-attested conformance is permitted.** Any implementer may
describe its implementation as "SADAR conformant" (or substantially
similar wording) as a self-attested, referential claim, provided the
claim is truthful, does not use either logo, and does not state or imply
that the implementation is "SADAR Certified," "SADAR Authorized,"
assessed, or endorsed.

**6.3. No implied certification or authorization.** Absent a written
grant under the Charter's programs, no party may use the terms "SADAR
Certified" or "SADAR Authorized," the SADAR logos, or any wording that
implies certification, authorization, assessment, or endorsement by
Cognita or OpenSemantics.org.

**6.4. Non-conformant implementations.** An implementation that does not
satisfy all normative requirements of the specification must not be
described as SADAR conformant or SADAR compliant.

**6.5. Forks and derivative works.** The Community Specification License
1.0 permits forks and derivative specifications. A fork or derivative may
state, truthfully and with attribution, that it is "based on" or "derived
from" SADAR, provided it clearly identifies itself as a separate work. It
must not be described as SADAR, SADAR conformant, certified, or
authorized, must not use the SADAR logos, and must not use the SADAR name
in a manner that implies it is the SADAR specification or is endorsed,
certified, or stewarded by Cognita or OpenSemantics.org.

---

## 7. Prohibited Uses

Without prior written authorization, you may not:

- use any of the Marks as, or as part of, your own company, product,
  service, or specification name;
- register or use the Marks, or confusingly similar terms, in domain
  names, social-media handles, or application names in a way that implies
  official status;
- use the Marks in a manner that implies partnership, sponsorship, or
  endorsement by Cognita or OpenSemantics.org;
- modify, abbreviate, translate, or create derivative forms of the Marks
  or logos; or
- use the Marks in a manner that is disparaging or misleading, or that
  dilutes or tarnishes them.

---

## 8. Use of the OpenSemantics.org Name

The OpenSemantics.org name may be used referentially to identify the
program that publishes the SADAR specification, subject to the same
conditions as Section 5. It may not be used to imply that a third party
operates, speaks for, or is endorsed by the program.

---

## 9. Authorization

Authorization to use a SADAR logo is requested through, and granted under,
the programs described in the Charter (Section 7). Authorization, if
granted, is in writing, is specific to the implementation and
specification version assessed, is non-exclusive and non-transferable,
and is revocable as set out in Section 10.

---

## 10. Enforcement, Revocation, and Reservation of Rights

**10.1.** Cognita may revoke any logo authorization upon misuse of the
Marks, failure to maintain conformance, or breach of the authorization
terms or this Policy.

**10.2.** Use of the ™ designation rather than ® reflects the current
common-law status of the Marks and does not waive or limit Cognita's
rights. Cognita reserves all rights in the Marks under applicable law.

**10.3.** Cognita may update its enforcement practices and may pursue the
remedies available to it for unauthorized or infringing use.

---

## 11. Changes to this Policy

The Steward may amend this Policy. Amendments are published in the
repository and are effective on publication.

Questions about this Policy, and requests for logo authorization, may be
